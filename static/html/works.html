About Us

Who Cares

The main motivation behind creating this platform and web application is to develop a practical, easy to use interface for understanding the decision-making process of complicated machine learning models. With an emphasis on how input features influence classification, we dive deeply into hidden layers' interpretation of inputs to better understand feature importance. With at least two different visualization tools for tabular and image data respectively, we provide users with the ability to easily and intuitively understand the AI decision making process. We began with existing work, by translating models from published papers into our backend stack. From there, we explored new approaches to the problem that aim to provide new insights and different perspectives. At a high level, the platform serves as a tool for comparing different types of models. Furthermore, we provide a framework for new models to be created and incorporated into our already existing platform, which makes it highly extensible.

Our Process

Through research, brainstorming and design we came to decisions about how to best present the data to users. We originally intended the service to be more for researchers, but eventually found a strong need in the general population to have AI knowledge and understand. This realization shaped the second half of our project to focus more on users with no background knowledge and tailor the website to them.

Explainable was started in Fall of 2019 at Tufts University.

This is being completed for the Senior Capstone class.
